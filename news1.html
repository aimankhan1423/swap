IFAT India

The world’s leading network for environmental technologies – IFAT India

IFAT success

The IFAT story is one of staggering success. It began in Munich in 1966 and now encompasses the entire world. Visitor numbers have increased from a starting figure of 10,200 to over 142,000 today.

Facts and Figures

IFAT's exhibitor and visitor figures, with 3,305 exhibitors from 58 countries and regions and 142,472 visitors from 162 nations coming together over an exhibition space of 260,000 square meters.

Supporting programs

Supporting programs

It aims bringing together solution providers, practitioners to share knowledge and experience that pertains to proven and innovative environmental technologies especially in the water and waste sector.

IFAT India has grown to be the most comprehensive platform for industry stakeholders.

ABOUT

The fair covered the areas of e-waste and waste-to-energy and was well complemented by the extensive supporting program. “The e-waste sector in India is growing rapidly, creating a demand for environmentally sound e-waste recycling and thereby opening up a lot of opportunities in green jobs as well as technical innovations and entrepreneurship,” says Ms. Deepali Sinha Khetriwal, Managing Director of Sofies Sustainability Leaders Pvt. Ltd. Also on the agenda were sustainable solutions and best-practices for development of urban infrastructure in India.

GOAL

The big goal behind it: a world without waste, in which used resources are recycled and remain in the cycle. This not only applies to solid waste but also to our sewage, which hides treasures such as phosphorus and nitrogen. These can be used, for example, to produce fertilizer.
