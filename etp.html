Effluent treatment plant

The effluent water treatment plant cleans the contaminated water excreted by industrial units. The water contains a large number of impurities, chemicals and other elements that are extremely harmful for the health of humans and animals. Also, there are long-term environmental impacts of the same. We bring energy-efficient water treatment system that reduces BOD and COD percentage up to 90 percent. Being a leading effluent treatment plant manufacturer, we offer systems that can serve a wide spectrum of industries such as dying, leather, paper, sugar, glass, chemicals, and fertilizers and so on.



Important components of Effluent Treatment Plant

    1. Bar Screen


    You don’t want physical impurities of large size in the water as they can damage the system and cause a breakdown. Bar screens prevent objects such as bricks, stones, plastic garbage, and rags entering the system. The process is important for successful operation of the plant.


    2. Dissolved Air Flotation


    In this process, air is dissolved in the water with tremendous pressure. When the air is released from the water in a basin or floatation tank, millions of tiny bubbles get attached to the suspended matter. Thus, it floats to the surface where skimming device removes it from the water.


    3. Dosing process


    Water is pumped to a tank which is called Dosing Tank. Here, the right quantities of dosing chemicals are sent at the optimum rate. It ensures that the plant gives long-term service in an optimized manner.


    4. Clarifier system I


    It is also called the Primary Clarifier. It clarifies the semi-treated water with the help of chemicals. Two parameters are important for effectiveness of clarifying process; retention time and the efficiency of plate settlers. The sludge gets accumulated in the sludge holding tank. Later it is taken to the Bop Reactor.


    5. Bio Reactor – I


    From this point, the biological treatment process starts. Submersible pumps shift the wastewater to the reactor. Eco diffusers and Bio Blocks start working on the wastewater. Diffusers aerate it so that microorganisms multiply and act on the organic stuff present in the water. As they consume the organic part, the overall organic constituents get degraded. The wastewater flows into the next chamber which is called Clarifier II.


    6. Clarifier II


    Secondary Clarifier or Clarifier II settles down the solids using the plate settler arrangement. The sludge is collected into the Sludge Holding Tank, and the clarified water is moved to another important chamber which is Bio Reactor II.


    7. Bio Rector II


    Here also, you fins Eco Bio blocks that treat the remaining organic matter under aerobic conditions. Like Bio Reactor I, here also diffused air accelerates the microbial growth. Here, the entire organic content gets degraded, and the clean water moves to Clarifier III. Clarifier II or Tertiary Clarifier
    The third Clarifier settles the solids by plate settler method. Like earlier processes, the sludge gets accumulated in the collection tanks. Clarified water is sent to the next part of the process, Filter Feed Tank.


    8. Filter Feed Tank


    Filter feed tank holds the clarified water and pumps it to the filtration chamber.


    9. Filtration Chamber


    Filtration chamber has sand and carbon filters to reduce the odor and suspended impurities from the water. From this point, the water moves to disinfection chamber.


    10. Disinfection Chamber


    You may use Ozone disinfection or UV disinfection, depending on the design specs of the treatment plant. It is a critical process that prevents the outbreak of dangerous infectious diseases. Water released from the Disinfection chamber is safe for the environment and humans or animals.


    11. Treated water tank


    Treated water gets stored here, and it can be used for several applications such as flushing, gardening, and industrial or construction purpose. The water is perfectly ok for the disposal in water bodies or underground.


    12. Sludge drying beds


    The beds dry the sludge received from the Primary and Secondary Clarifier and Settling tank. The dried sludge is used in several applications.
